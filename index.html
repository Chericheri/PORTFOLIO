About Me

I’m a selftaught developer who swapped late-night fashion runway call times for late night debugging sessions though the espresso intake never changed. My lens has always been human first: whether I’m sketching a Dior-inspired pattern or architecting a React component tree, I ask “how will this make someone feel?”

My journey started backstage with measuring tape and mood-boards; today I measure performance budgets and craft pixel-perfect, accessible interfaces. I thrive on turning knotty problems into elegant solutions that look as good at 320 px as they do on a 4 K runway screen.

Off screen you’ll still find me on mountain switchbacks chasing sunrise shots, at casting calls perfecting posture, or behind a sewing machine learning how to turn sketches into stitches—because code and couture both run on precision, creativity, and a fearless willingness to unpick what isn’t working.
